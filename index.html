🛠️ Routine Microwave Maintenance

01

Clean the Interior Weekly

Mix 1 cup water + 1 tbsp vinegar in a microwave-safe bowl. Heat for 5 minutes. The steam loosens grease and food residue. Wipe thoroughly with a damp cloth. Pay special attention to the ceiling — splatter buildup can cause arcing.

02

Inspect & Clean the Door Seal

The rubber door gasket prevents microwave radiation leakage. Wipe with a damp cloth monthly. Check for cracks, hardening, or deformation. A damaged seal must be replaced immediately — never operate with a broken door seal.

03

Clean the Air Vents

Blocked vents cause overheating and premature component failure. Use a soft brush or compressed air to clear vents on the back and sides every 3 months. Ensure at least 3 inches of clearance around the unit.

04

Test the Turntable & Roller Ring

Remove the glass tray and roller ring. Wash both in warm soapy water. Inspect the roller guide for cracks. Ensure the coupling gear is intact and properly seated. Reassemble and test rotation.

05

Check the Waveguide Cover

This cardboard or mica cover protects the magnetron from food splatter. If it's burnt, cracked or food-soaked, replace it. Replacements cost very little and are model-specific — always use genuine parts.

⚠️

HIGH VOLTAGE WARNING

Microwave capacitors can store up to 2,100 volts even when unplugged. Always discharge the capacitor before touching internal components. If you are not experienced with electronics, contact a certified technician for internal repairs.

🛠️ Seasonal AC Maintenance Plan

  • ✔ Clean or replace air filters — dirty filters reduce efficiency by up to 15%
  • ✔ Check and clean the indoor unit's front grille and vents
  • ✔ Test thermostat and remote control operation
  • ✔ Listen for unusual sounds during startup and operation
  • ✔ Check drain pan for standing water or mold growth
  • ✔ Ensure outdoor unit is free of debris (leaves, grass, dirt)
  • ✔ Clean evaporator and condenser coils with coil cleaner spray
  • ✔ Flush the condensate drain line with a bleach-water solution
  • ✔ Check refrigerant lines for insulation damage or ice buildup
  • ✔ Inspect electrical connections and tighten if needed
  • ✔ Clean the blower fan wheel of dust and debris
  • ✔ Lubricate fan motor bearings if accessible
  • ✔ Professional refrigerant level check and top-up if needed
  • ✔ Full electrical system inspection by a certified technician
  • ✔ Compressor performance and amperage test
  • ✔ Check capacitors and contactors in outdoor unit
  • ✔ Full coil deep-clean with professional equipment
  • ✔ Inspect and seal ductwork for energy efficiency
⚠️

REFRIGERANT HANDLING — CERTIFIED ONLY

Refrigerant handling (R-410A, R-32, R-22) is regulated by law and requires EPA Section 608 certification. Never attempt to recharge refrigerant yourself. Improper handling causes environmental damage, equipment failure, and personal injury. Always hire a licensed HVAC technician for refrigerant work.

🛡️ Safety

Safety First — Always

Before you pick up any tool, read and follow these critical safety rules.

1

Always Unplug First

Disconnect from mains power before opening any appliance. Wait at least 60 seconds after unplugging a microwave before accessing internals.

2

Discharge Capacitors

Microwave capacitors hold lethal voltage even hours after unplugging. Use a properly rated discharge tool before touching any internal components.

3

Work Dry & Insulated

Never work in wet conditions. Wear rubber-soled shoes and insulated gloves when working near electrical components.

4

Never Bypass Safety Switches

Microwave door interlock switches and AC pressure switches exist to protect you. Bypassing them even temporarily can be fatal.

5

Use Genuine Parts

Always replace with OEM or equivalent-spec parts. Substandard components can fail dangerously, cause fires, or void your warranty.

6

Know Your Limits

If you're unsure at any point, stop and call a certified technician. Saving money is never worth risking your life or your home.
